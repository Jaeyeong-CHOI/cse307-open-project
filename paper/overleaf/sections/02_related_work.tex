\section{Related Work}
Prior discussions on coding assistants report mixed outcomes between speed and skill formation. Some recent reports suggest that heavy assistant usage can reduce debugging quality for less experienced developers, though findings depend strongly on task design and participant profile \citep{anthropic2026coding}.

Our study differs in that it isolates grammar-compliance behavior under controlled syntax transformations. Instead of measuring only pass/fail on canonical tasks, we measure whether models respect transformed language rules and how often they revert to dominant Python priors.
