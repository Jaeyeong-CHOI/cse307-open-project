\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Code-generation benchmarks and robustness}
Code-generation benchmarks and model baselines (e.g., HumanEval and MBPP) motivate short-horizon program synthesis evaluation.
\cite{chen2021codex,austin2021program}

\subsection{Code representation models under perturbation}
Pretraining work such as \textit{CodeBERT}, \textit{CodeT5}, and \textit{UniXcoder} improves code representation transfer, while preserving language semantics under transformed-token constraints remains underexplored in these settings.
\cite{feng2020codebert,wang2021codet5,guo2022unixcoder}

\subsection{Instruction and alignment effects}
Instruction-following tuning improves broad task compliance, yet its effect on non-standard syntax transformations has not been studied sufficiently.
\cite{ouyang2022instruct,brown2020fewshot}

\subsection{Repair-oriented generation}
Editing and infilling methods (e.g., InCoder) indicate that bidirectional context can support local correction, relevant to our roundtrip diagnosis design.
\cite{fried2022incoder}

\subsection{Software-engineering realism}
SWE-bench demonstrates the gap between benchmark-scale success and real-world patching complexity, reinforcing our emphasis on strict artifact and lineage control.
\cite{jimenez2024swebench}

\subsection{Constraint-aware decoding}
Recent progress in constrained decoding suggests adding semantic constraints can complement syntax-layer methods, an important extension direction for confusion-language pipelines.
\cite{muendler2025type}
