\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Code generation foundations}
Early code-generation benchmarks and model families (e.g., HumanEval and MBPP) frame our problem context.\citep{chen2021codex,austin2021program}
\
\subsection{Model robustness under constrained syntax}
Recent LLM code models (e.g., pretraining methods such as \textit{CodeBERT}, \textit{CodeT5}, \textit{UniXcoder}) have improved language-to-code transfer, but the gap between learned natural-language priors and constrained syntax remains underexplored.\citep{feng2020codebert,wang2021codet5,guo2022unixcoder}
\subsection{Instruction following and alignment}
Instruction-tuning and human-feedback approaches increased compliance for broad tasks, yet their effect on syntax-preserving transforms has not been systematically validated under alias perturbation settings.\citep{ouyang2022instruct,brown2020fewshot}
\subsection{Repair and edit-style generation}
Studies on infilling/editing architectures (e.g., InCoder) suggest that bidirectional context can help fix local code regions, which is relevant to our roundtrip-based mismatch diagnosis pipeline.\citep{fried2022incoder}
\subsection{Task realism and software-engineering benchmarks}
Beyond short-function benchmarks, SWE-bench shows that real-world patching tasks remain far more difficult and expose hidden fragility in supposedly strong models, motivating strict schema and lineage controls for our pipeline.\citep{jimenez2024swebench}
\subsection{Type/constraint-aware generation}
Emerging work on type-constrained decoding indicates a promising direction for enforcing semantic validity during generation, complementing our syntax-focused alias-control strategy.\citep{muendler2025type}
